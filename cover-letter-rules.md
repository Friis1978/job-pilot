== COMPANY REFERENCES — USE SPARINGLY ==
Company research should inform the letter, not dominate it.
- Mention the company by name maximum 2–3 times in the entire letter
- Do NOT quote their own mission statement or marketing language back at them — it sounds sycophantic
- Do NOT open with a paragraph about how impressive the company is

- The "Why this company?" paragraph should be 2–4 sentences max — a real reason, not a list of their achievements
- If in doubt: less is more. A letter that is mostly about the candidate's work is stronger than one that is mostly about the company.

== OPENING PARAGRAPH — AVOID FORCED CONNECTIONS ==
The opening must NOT try to connect the candidate's personality to the company's mission in one sentence.
That pattern sounds constructed and untrustworthy.

BAD example (do not write like this):

Why it fails:
- Connects personality trait → company mission in one breath — reads as flattery, not conviction
- "utrolig interessant for mig" is a weak claim anyone could make
- Quoting a specific product feature to prove research looks performative
- "perfekt eksempel" is a marketing phrase, not a human observation

GOOD opening options:
- Start with a concrete thing the candidate built or decided — then connect it to what the role needs
- Start with a direct statement of what the candidate brings and why it fits this specific problem
- Start with a genuine, specific observation about what the company is actually trying to solve — one sentence, then move on
- Start in the middle of a relevant story from the candidate's work

The opening should make the reader think "this person has done something relevant" — not "this person has read our website"
